I see that YOU are looking to buy one of those beasts? Well, it ain't easy, considering their age and rarity (and WEATHER conditions)


The following will apply to all models, so choose your weapon carefully


  • Engine:
    • Before 2003, 230 engines were burning oil due to oil ring design. Look for oil leaks and smoke.
    • Make sure engine starts every time. Drive the car for some time and see if she is overheating or not
    • See who you are buying from: a youngster will most likely trash the car around so the engine will go out sooner
    • If you are after any of the GT-Four - a good sign is a stock intercooler. Turbotimer is a nice feature, if present
  • Body:
    • Inspect the whole body for dents or traces of repair, i.e. fresh paint or brand new parts
    • Check the bottom of the door seals - look out for rust
    • If after 165 - check the boot for any rust signs
    • Go for original exterior, as it will have the biggest value over time - for you and for car enthusiasts
  • Suspension:
    • If buying from UK or NI - do make sure there is no rust underneath
    • Check shock absorber rubber boots - make sure there are no holes or punctures
      • WARNING! - If you are buying a 205 - make sure that Super Strut suspension is in top notch condition, because replacing it will cost around 2k euro
    • 185 is a more forgiving car, so try shocks with your own weight. See if springs are intact
    • For 165 you will have to go with aftermarket solutions, because this model is out of production for a long time

My advice is to look out for cars in the following order:

  1. ST205 - powerfull and there are plenty of parts available
  2. T230 - loads of well-looked after examples out there. Plenty of maintanance parts available.
  3. ST185 - rare, powerfull and expensive. For car lovers.
  4. ST165 - for enthusiats only. No parts or manuals available. Everything is aftermarket.
